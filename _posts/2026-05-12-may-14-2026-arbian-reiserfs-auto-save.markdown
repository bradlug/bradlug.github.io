<p>With only three people in attendance, the meeting mostly consisted of gentle conversation.</p><p><strong>David</strong> unboxed an extension lead for a Micro SD which avoids having to insert Micro SDs into fiddly slots. There are now FT serial port to USB adapters for the <a href="http://www.orangepi.org/orangepiwiki/index.php/Orange_Pi_4_LTS" type="text/html" role="link">Orange Pi4 LTS</a>, the <a href="https://libre.computer/products/aml-s905x-cc/" type="text/html" role="link">Libre Computer Board AML-S905X-CC</a>, otherwise known as ‘Le potato’ and Raspberry Pi 3. <a href="https://armbian.com/" type="text/html" role="link">Armbian</a> seems to be one that everything coalesces around. It supports 339 boards and some producers offer Platinum support. They have a fully open build system creating images which are reproducible. However, you have to download firmware blobs from manufacturers and they are hardly ever updated. A particular blob cannot use older firmware.</p><p><strong>Darren</strong> asked about moving things from a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ReiserFS" type="text/html" role="link">ReiserFS</a> partition and it was pointed out that ReiserFS was supported in Linux up to 6.12. Also <a href="https://gparted.org/" type="text/html" role="link">GParted</a> recognises it.</p><p><strong>John</strong> mentioned that, when trying to help a Windows user, he had found that auto-save now saves to the cloud and so needs in Internet connection. His brother had explained to him that the old Microsoft auto-save is called auto-recover as it just saves what is needed to recover a file whereas the new auto-save saves to a separate file.</p><p>John commented that that explained a difference between <a href="https://www.lyx.org/" type="text/html" role="link">LyX</a> and <a href="https://www.libreoffice.org/" type="text/html" role="link">LibreOffice</a>. LyX has always auto-saved but to a local file, not one on the cloud, whereas LibreOffice, in Microsoft’s new terminology, auto-recovers files if necessary.</p>
